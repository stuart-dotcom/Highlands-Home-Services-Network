What Defines a "Verified Opportunity"

Every lead delivered through our network must pass all three of the following criteria before it is routed to you:

  • Confirmed Location — The homeowner's address is verified within Highlands County (Sebring, Avon Park, Lake Placid, Lorida, or Venus) before routing.
  • Active Service Intent — The homeowner has described a specific, current need for your trade. We screen for genuine requests — not general browsing.
  • Validated Contact Data — The submission includes a working phone number or email address confirmed at the point of inquiry.

A lead is not evaluated on whether the homeowner ultimately hires a contractor, accepts a quote, or proceeds with the project. We deliver the verified connection — you close the job.

Billing policy: A lead is considered delivered and billable when verified contact information and a confirmed service request are transmitted to you — regardless of whether the job is quoted, scheduled, or completed. Billing occurs after delivery, never in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to pay for a lead if I don't close the job?

Yes. Our service is to provide you with a Verified Opportunity — a homeowner in your service area with a specific, current need and validated contact information.

A lead is considered delivered and billable when verified contact information and a confirmed service request are transmitted to you — regardless of whether the job is quoted, scheduled, or completed.

While we provide the exclusive connection, the responsibility for quoting, following up, and closing the contract remains with you.

This policy is stated at setup and reflected in your contractor agreement. By submitting your application, you acknowledge that billing is based on verified lead delivery, not job outcome.

How do you define a "Verified Lead"?

A lead is considered verified when it meets all three of the following criteria:

  • Confirmed Location — The homeowner's address is within Highlands County.
  • Service Match — The request specifically matches your licensed trade.
  • Validated Contact Data — The submission includes a working phone number or email address.

A lead is not evaluated on whether the homeowner ultimately hires a contractor, accepts a quote, or proceeds with the project. We deliver the verified connection — the sale is yours to close.

Why is exclusive routing better for me?

Most lead generation platforms sell the same homeowner's name to 3–5 different contractors simultaneously, forcing you to compete on price alone and race to the phone.

Highlands Home Services Network routes each lead to one contractor only. That homeowner's contact information is yours and yours alone — no other pro in the network receives it. This gives you the highest possible chance of closing the job at your full professional rate, without a bidding war.

When and how do I get billed?

You are never billed in advance. Billing happens only after a verified lead has been delivered to you via your preferred method — SMS, email, or phone call.

We invoice you at the per-lead rate agreed upon during your setup. There are no surprise charges, no tiered fees applied retroactively, and no billing before delivery.

Is there a monthly membership or sign-up fee?

No. We operate on a pure pay-per-performance model. There are no monthly retainers, no hidden administrative fees, and no long-term contracts.

If we don't send you leads, you don't pay us anything.

What if the homeowner requests a quote before committing to hire?

Every lead we deliver has passed our intent screening — confirmed Highlands County location, a specific trade request, and validated contact information. What happens after delivery is a sales conversation, not a lead quality issue.

A homeowner requesting a quote is a normal, healthy part of the hiring process. Your response speed, professionalism, and pricing are the variables that determine the outcome from that point forward. A prompt, professional estimate is your most effective tool for converting an inquiry into a booked job.
