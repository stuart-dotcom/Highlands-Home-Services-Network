From request to routed lead

From sourcing homeowner leads to getting them on your phone.

Here’s exactly what happens at every step when a Highlands County homeowner requests service through Highlands Home Services Network.

1. We attract Highlands County homeowners.

Highlands Home Services Network is not a contractor. We are a digital lead generation and referral service. Our job is to find local homeowners who need a licensed tradesperson — and connect them with the right contractor in our network.

We operate homeowner-facing digital properties targeting Highlands County residents searching for home services — Sebring, Avon Park, Lake Placid, Lorida, and Venus.

When a homeowner submits a service request, it enters our system with their contact details, service need, and location.

2. We screen and qualify every request.

Every inquiry is screened for confirmed Highlands County location, genuine service intent, and valid contact information. Submissions that don’t qualify are discarded — never sold.

You never compete on the same lead. You never pay for obvious tire-kickers. You’re never locked into a long-term contract.

3. We match each lead to one contractor.

Once a lead passes screening it’s matched to the single network contractor whose service area covers that homeowner. It is never sent to multiple contractors.

When you receive a lead, you get full homeowner contact info — name, address, phone, and email — everything you need to follow up immediately.

Delivered in real time via phone, SMS, or email — your preference. Emergency leads can trigger an immediate call in addition to digital delivery.

4. You close the job. We invoice for leads.

You invoice the homeowner directly for the work performed. We invoice you only for verified leads delivered at the pay-per-lead rate agreed at setup.

There are no monthly retainers, no ad budgets to manage, and no long-term software contracts. You can scale volume up or down as your schedule allows.

Once routed to you, no other contractor in the network receives that homeowner’s information.

Contractor onboarding at a glance.

You submit your application with your Florida trade license number and the Highlands County cities you cover. We verify your license against the Florida DBPR database before approving your account.

Once your application is approved and your service area is set up, you can begin receiving leads as soon as qualified homeowner requests come in within your coverage area.

A lead constitutes delivery of screened homeowner contact information that meets our standards for location, intent, and valid contact details. We do not guarantee job conversion rates, but we do filter for genuine service needs.
